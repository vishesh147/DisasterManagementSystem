Disaster Safety Guidelines

Earthquakes

  • Drop, cover, and hold on: Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy table or desk if possible, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay away from windows and heavy furniture: Glass and objects can break and fall during an earthquake.
  • Evacuate tall buildings and structures: After the shaking stops, evacuate tall buildings and structures as soon as it is safe to do so.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Have a disaster supply kit that includes food, water, first-aid supplies, and other essential items in case of an earthquake.

Hurricanes and tropical storms

  • Evacuate if advised: If you are in a coastal area and the authorities issue an evacuation order, leave as soon as possible.
  • Secure your property: Take steps to secure your property and protect it from potential damage. This may include covering windows and doors, storing outdoor furniture and equipment, and removing loose items from your yard.
  • Have a disaster supply kit: Have a disaster supply kit that includes food, water, first-aid supplies, a battery-powered radio, flashlights, and other essential items.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any new warnings or evacuation orders by listening to local news and following official social media accounts.

Tornadoes

  • Take cover: If you are indoors, go to a basement, storm cellar, or an interior room on the lowest floor. If you are outdoors, try to find a low-lying area or seek shelter in a sturdy building.
  • Stay away from windows: Windows can break during a tornado, so try to stay away from them.
  • Protect your head: Cover your head with your hands or a pillow to protect yourself from falling debris.
  • Evacuate mobile homes: Mobile homes are not safe during tornadoes, so evacuate to a nearby building or shelter if possible.

Floods

  • Evacuate if advised: If you are in a flood-prone area and the authorities issue an evacuation order, leave as soon as possible.
  • Stay away from floodwaters: Do not attempt to walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters. The water may be contaminated or contain dangerous debris.
  • Turn off utilities: If you are instructed to evacuate, turn off your utilities before leaving. This will reduce the risk of fire or electrocution.
  • Have a disaster supply kit: Have a disaster supply kit that includes food, water, first-aid supplies, a battery-powered radio, flashlights, and other essential items.

Wildfires

  • Evacuate if advised: If you are in an area affected by a wildfire and the authorities issue an evacuation order, leave as soon as possible.
  • Close windows and doors: Close windows and doors to reduce the amount of smoke that enters your home.
  • Turn off air conditioning: If you are advised to evacuate, turn off your air conditioning to reduce the risk of fire.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any new evacuation orders by listening to local news and following official social media accounts.

Drought Safety Guidelines

  • Conserve water: Take steps to conserve water, such as fixing leaks, taking shorter showers, and only watering plants when necessary.
  • Plant drought-resistant plants: Consider planting drought-resistant plants in your yard, which require less water to thrive.
  • Be mindful of outdoor activities: Limit outdoor activities that use water, such as washing cars and filling pools, during a drought.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any water restrictions by following local news and official social media accounts.

Landslide Safety Guidelines

  • Avoid unstable areas: If you live in an area that is prone to landslides, avoid building or camping in unstable areas or near steep slopes.
  • Listen for warning signs: Listen for warning signs of a landslide, such as cracking or popping sounds, and evacuate immediately if you hear them.
  • Be aware of mudslides: Be aware of mudslides, which can occur during or after heavy rains, and evacuate immediately if you see one approaching.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.

Tsunami Safety Guidelines

  • Evacuate if advised: If you are in a coastal area and a tsunami warning is issued, evacuate immediately to higher ground.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Stay away from the beach: Stay away from the beach and stay out of the water during a tsunami warning, as dangerous waves can suddenly reach the shore.
  • Check for damage: After a tsunami, check for damage and stay away from any damaged or flooded areas until it is safe to return.

Volcanic Eruption Safety Guidelines

  • Evacuate if advised: If you are in an area that is near an active volcano and an eruption is imminent, evacuate immediately to a safe location.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid ash: Avoid breathing in ash, which can irritate the eyes, skin, and respiratory system, by staying indoors and sealing doors, windows, and air vents.
  • Protect your vehicle: Protect your vehicle from ash by covering it with a tarp or blanket and avoiding driving in areas where ash is falling heavily.

Industrial Accidents

  • Evacuate: If you are near an industrial accident, evacuate immediately to a safe location, following the directions of first responders.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid contact: Avoid contact with any hazardous materials that may have been released, including any liquids, gases, or vapors, by staying upwind and uphill of the accident site.
  • Shelter in place: If instructed to do so by first responders, shelter in place by closing all windows and doors, turning off all ventilation systems, and sealing cracks and other openings with duct tape and plastic sheeting.

Oil Spills

  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid contact: Avoid contact with any oil that has been spilled, which can be toxic and flammable, by staying away from the spill site.
  • Protect wildlife: Protect wildlife that may have been affected by the spill by reporting any oiled wildlife to the appropriate authorities.
  • Cooperate with cleanup efforts: If you live or work near an oil spill, cooperate with cleanup efforts by following any instructions from first responders and cleanup crews.

Nuclear Accidents

  • Evacuate: If you are near a nuclear accident, evacuate immediately to a safe location, following the directions of first responders.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid exposure: Avoid exposure to any radioactive material that may have been released, by staying away from the accident site and any contaminated areas.
  • Protect your food and water: Protect your food and water from contamination by following any instructions from first responders and local health authorities.

Transportation Accidents

  • Evacuate: If you are near a transportation accident, evacuate immediately to a safe location, following the directions of first responders.
  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid the area: Avoid the area of the transportation accident, as it may be hazardous and subject to further incidents or hazards, such as fire or explosion.
  • Render aid: If safe to do so, render aid to any injured individuals, following appropriate safety procedures and protocols.
  • Cooperate with first responders: Cooperate with first responders and other authorities at the scene, following any instructions or requests for information or assistance.

  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid the area: Avoid the area of the infrastructure failure, as it may be hazardous and subject to further incidents or hazards, such as falling debris or additional collapses.
  • Render aid: If safe to do so, render aid to any injured individuals, following appropriate safety procedures and protocols.
  • Cooperate with first responders: Cooperate with first responders and other authorities at the scene, following any instructions or requests for information or assistance.

Pandemics

  • Wash your hands frequently: Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in a public place or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.
  • Wear a mask: Wear a mask in public settings, especially when social distancing is difficult to maintain.
  • Avoid close contact: Avoid close contact with people who are sick and stay home if you are feeling ill.
  • Cover your mouth and nose: Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you cough or sneeze, and dispose of the tissue immediately.
  • Clean and disinfect: Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces daily, such as doorknobs and light switches.

Epidemic Outbreaks

  • Stay informed: Stay informed about the latest developments and any evacuation orders by following local news and official social media accounts.
  • Avoid the area: Avoid the area of the epidemic outbreak, if possible, as it may be hazardous and subject to further incidents or hazards.
  • Practice good hygiene: Practice good hygiene, including washing your hands frequently, covering your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, and avoiding close contact with sick individuals.
  • Cooperate with authorities: Cooperate with health authorities and other authorities, following any instructions or requests for information or assistance.
  • Seek medical attention: If you develop symptoms of the epidemic, seek medical attention immediately, following the instructions of your healthcare provider.

Food Contamination

  • Check for recalls: Check for any food recalls related to the contaminated food item, following official sources such as the FDA or USDA.
  • Discard contaminated food: Discard any contaminated food items, following the instructions of the recall or health authorities.
  • Seek medical attention: If you have consumed contaminated food and are experiencing symptoms, seek medical attention immediately, following the instructions of your healthcare provider.
  • Report the contamination: Report the contamination to the relevant authorities, such as the FDA or USDA, following the instructions provided on their websites or through their reporting mechanisms.

Water Contamination

  • Check for boil advisories: Check for any boil advisories related to the contaminated water source, following official sources such as the local water utility or health department.
  • Boil or use bottled water: If a boil advisory is in effect, boil the water or use bottled water for drinking, cooking, and brushing teeth.
  • Seek medical attention: If you have consumed contaminated water and are experiencing symptoms, seek medical attention immediately, following the instructions of your healthcare provider.
  • Report the contamination: Report the contamination to the relevant authorities, such as the local water utility or health department, following the instructions provided on their websites or through their reporting mechanisms.
